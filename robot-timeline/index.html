Queen Elizabeth

Britain’s Longest Reigning Monarch

1952

George VI dies aged 56. His daughter, Princess Elizabeth, on hearing the news returns from a trip to Kenya. She is proclaimed Queen of the United Kingdom at the age of 25.

1953

Queen Elizabeth II is crowned at a coronation ceremony in Westminster Abbey in front of more than 8,000 guests. The Queen promises "e;throughout all my life and with all my heart I shall strive to be worthy of your trust"e;.

1953

Queen Elizabeth embarks on her first Commonweath tour. Travelling by air, sea and rail, she tours the world for almost six months. She visits 15 Commonwealth countries as Queen in the 1950s alone.

1960

Prince Andrew is born. Becoming the Duke Of York, Andrew is the first birth for a reigning monarch in 103 years.

1964

Prince Edward is born. Becoming the Earl of Wessex, he is the youngest of the family. His brother Charles and sister Anne were born before the Queen’s reign began.

1977

More than a million people line the streets to celebrate the Queen’s silver jubilee, marking 25 years of her reign.

1981

Prince Charles marries Diana Spencer at St Paul’s Cathedral. An estimated 750 million tune in globally to watch the event.

1982

A man breaks into Buckingham Palace, spending 10 minutes talking to the Queen in her bedroom. Michael Fagan is arrested for trespass and stealing half a bottle of wine.

1982

Windsor Fire!

1992

The Queen’s ‘Annus Horribillis’, as her daughter Princess Anne divorces, and the Prince of Wales and Duke of York seperate from their partners. Windsor castle goes up in flames. ‘1992 is not a year I shall look back with undiluted pleasure’ she says.

1997

Princess Diana dies in a car crash in Paris. The Queen says she is ‘deeply shocked and distressed’, but at first chooses to stay at Balmoral Castle in Scotland.

1997

After criticism of her handling of Diana’s death, the Queen returns to London and addresses the public.

2002

Both the Queen’s sister Princess Margaret and the Queen Mother die. The Queen is at her mother’s bedside when the 101-year-old passes away.

2002

The Queen celebrates her Golden Jubilee, marking 50 years of her reign. She travels over 40,000 miles across the Commonwealth to mark the event.

2011

The Queen’s grandson Prince William marries Catherine Middleton. He is the second of the Queen’s grandchildren to marry.

2011

The Queen embarks on the first state visit to Ireland since the country gained independence.

2012

The Queen celebrates 60 years on the throne with a Diamond Jubilee River Pageant. 1,000 boats accompany the royal family along the Thames, as the weather does little to dampen the British public’s spirits.

2012

At the London 2012 Olympics the Queen jumps out of a helicopter to mark the Opening Ceremony. In fact, it is a stunt double, but embodies the jovial nature with which the games are treated by the nation.

2013

The Queen’s great-grandson Prince George is born, becoming fourth in line to the throne. Primogeniture was abolished in 2011, meaning had a baby girl been born first, she would have become Queen.

2015

Queen Elizabeth II becomes the longest reigning British monarch. She has surpassed Queen Victoria’s time on the throne at 23,226 days, 16 hours and 24 minutes.
