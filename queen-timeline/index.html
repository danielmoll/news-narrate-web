Queen Elizabeth

The World’s Longest Reigning Monarch

1952

George VI dies aged 56. His daughter, Princess Elizabeth, is proclaimed Queen of the United Kingdom.

1953

Queen Elizabeth II is crowned at a coronation ceremony in Westminster Abbey in front of more than 8,000 guests.

1953

Elizabeth embarks on her first Commonwealth tour. Travelling by air, sea and rail, she tours the world for almost six months.

1960

Prince Andrew is born. The now Duke of York becomes the first birth for a reigning monarch in 103 years.

1964

Prince Edward is born. Becoming the Earl of Wessex, he is the youngest of the family. His brother Charles and sister Anne were born before the Queen’s reign begins.

1974

The Queen’s daughter Princess Anne escapes an apparent kindnap attempt when the car she is travelling in is blocked by another vehicle.

1977

More than a million line the streets to celebrate the Queen’s silver jubilee, marking 25 years of her reign.

1981

Prince Charles marries Diana Spencer at St Paul’s Cathedral. An estimated 750 million tune in globally to watch the event.

1982

A man breaks into Buckingham Palace, spending 10 minutes talking to the Queen in her bedroom. Michael Fagan is arrested for trespass and stealing half a bottle of wine.

1982

Prince William is born at St Mary’s Hospital. He becomes third in line to the throne.

1992

The Queen’s ‘Annus Horribillis’, as her daughter Princess Anne divorces, and the Prince of Wales and Duke of York separate from their partners. Windsor Castle goes up in flames.

1992

Buckingham Palace is opened to the public for the first time. Ticket sales are used to cover the costs of Windsor Castle’s restoration.

1997

Princess Diana dies in a car crash in Paris. The Queen says she is ‘deeply shocked and distressed’, but at first chooses to stay at Balmoral Castle in Scotland.

1997

After criticism of her handling of Diana’s death, the Queen returns to London, addressing the public.

2002

Both the Queen’s sister Princess Margaret, and the Queen Mother die. The Queen is at her mother’s bedside when the 101 year old passes away.

2002

The Queen celebrates her Golden Jubilee, marking 50 years of her reign. She travels over 40,000 miles across the Commonwealth to mark the event.

2009

@BritishMonarchy starts tweeting, although the Queen does not send her first tweet from the account for another five years.

2011

The Queen embarks on the first state visit to Ireland since the country gained independence.

2011

Primogeniture is abolished. Female members of the Royal family now have equality with the male counterparts for ascension to the throne.

2012

The Queen celebrates 60 years on the throne with a Diamond Jubilee

2015

Queen Elizabeth II becomes the longest reigning British monarch. She has surpassed Queen Victoria’s time on the throne at 23,226 days, 16 hours and 24 minutes.
