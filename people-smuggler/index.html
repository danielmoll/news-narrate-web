Almost 150,000 migrants have crossed the med into Europe this year already. The 180 mile journey from Africa to one of Europe's southernmost points claims thousands of lives every year, but for the migrants braving the Sea, it is the final leg of a long, dangerous and expensive journey. Khartoum: Sudan

The start of many migrants journeys to Europe. A bustling metropolitan area of over five million people that is a magnet for refugees fleeing conflict in neighbouring countries. Migrants meet their handlers at the Omdurman Market and negotiate a price for the first stage of their journey north. The journey north starts here. For many, they'll never reach Europe.

Alex Crawford on the first steps migrants take on their journey from some of Africa's most troubled regions to Europe's land of opportunity.

Kufra

876 miles

Kufra was for centuries a staging post on slave routs. Today it is entry point for all sorts of contraband into Libya. Locals have tried to prevent the illicit trade into the town with The Great Wall Of Kufra stretching 150km around the town. It struggles to stop the flow of people north with the harsh reality of life in the Sahara and the well-equipped smugglers easily navigating over it.

How is an ancient stop on Africa's trading routes now under the control of people smugglers?

Smugglers store supplies and weaponry at hidden camps in the desert. The Libyan authorities will not take them on. Migrants say the Sahara desert is the first sea they have to cross if they have any chance of reaching Europe.

Benghazi

1495 miles

Libya's second city is the frontline of the country's civil war. Fighters from the National Army and the internationally-recognised Tobruk government clash with Islamic State fighters trying to spread the group's poisonous message across the Middle East. The dangerous conditions don't hinder the smugglers from moving men, women and children through the city.

Having been herded across deserts like human cattle, migrants now face being caught up in a civil war.

In fact, they provide the perfect anarchic conditions for people smuggling gangs smuggling to thrive.

Tripoli

2126 miles

Tripoli seems like any other city in the Middle East, a thriving bustling home millions of people. But it is a facade. A government unrecognised by the outside world is in charge and smugglers have a free-run in large areas of the city. They use dilapidated houses and old factories to store their cargo until passage can be arranged. The wait for the right sea conditions can take weeks. Sometimes though, the migrants who have travelled over two thousand miles to get here are simply be held until more money is wired to their smugglers.

Tripoli may look safe, but hidden below it's rooftops is a city in the grip of dangerous smugglers.

Zuwara

2199 miles

It should only take a few hours to get to Europe from Zuwara. A good boat should be able to make the journey in a single sunny Mediterranean afternoon. For the migrants desperate to cross to southern Europe, they face an arduous journey on rickety old boats that the smugglers can afford to lose. They are packed on board, with those who pay the least locked into the hold. Thousands leave Zuwara for Europe, but hundreds never get there.

It may be closer than ever but there is still one deadly step on the journey to Europe.

Hundreds never make it….the bodies washing up on the shore here and the local cemetery is packed.

Lampedusa

2379 miles

For the migrants who make it to Lampedusa, the journey is simply half-done. They may encoutner some smiling faces for the first time in weeks as medics and aid workers help them from the boat, but they now have to reach mainland Europe. They are exhausted and terrified, but for many the dream of a life in the comfort of northern Europe's major cities will spur them on to face another dangerous journey.

Setting foot on European soil may feel like the end of their journey, but smuggled migrants rapidly discover they are simply at the start of another difficult battle to get into Europe.
